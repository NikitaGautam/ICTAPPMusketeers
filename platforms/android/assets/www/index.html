Legal Procedure To Be Carried Out For Domestic Violence Victims

  • The victim can lodge a written/oral complaint with the Police Office, National Women's Commission or local body.

  • If written, the complaint is immediately taken into consideration, else it is first written down, then registered.

  • If the complaint is lodged in the National Women's Commission, necessary action shall be taken in accordance with the relevant law that governs the National Women's Commission.

  • If the complaint is lodged in Police Station / Local Body, the accused shall be held in custody within 24 hours. If the accused refuses to come, force shall be applied for arrest.

  • If the victim is injured, medical report is drawn up and victim taken to hospital.

  • If the victim wants, police / local body conducts reconciliation between two parties.

  • The police / local body ensure the presence of the prosecutor in court on the hearing day. If the prosecutor fails to be present, a written notice is sent to court.

  • The court makes the decision based on right ACT.

  • The victim can file order directly with the court. If the court has the reason to believe, the court shall opt for measures of protection to the victim.

Legal Procedure To Be Carried Out For Human Trafficking Victims

  • The victim can report to the nearest police station.

  • The victim can put written request to remain unnamed; the police office which registers the report should then maintain his/her confidentiality.

  • The police office should take the statement immediately. The victim shall then be taken to the nearest district court to certify the statement as soon as possible.

  • There are two possibilities based on your certification:

  • The district judge shall either certify the statement after reading it aloud and noting whether there is difference or not in the statement, or the court can take the certified statement as evidence even if the victim does not appear in the court in the course of further court proceeding.

  • The probable suspects will be taken into arrest and further investigation will take place.

  • Prosecution is brought into custody.

  • Burden of proof: The suspect tries to justify that he has not committed crime.

  • The victim can keep separate law practitioner to represent him/her.

  • The victim can keep translator if s/he speaks language other than the one used in court.

  • The court makes judgment based on evidences and hearings.
